Dry Ice Blasting Compared To Other Methods

There are many available methods for cleaning industrial equipment. Each method has advantages and drawbacks, and some are comparable to dry ice blasting in some areas while falling short in others. These are the differences between other cleaning techniques and dry ice blasting.

  • Abrasive blasting. Abrasive blasting can be dangerous and toxic for operators, unlike dry ice blasting. To avoid being exposed to fumes or dust, the operator should wear a mask and a breathing device.
  • Soda blasting. Abrasive blasting can be used in a variety of ways, including soda blasting.
  • You can pressure wash. Pressure washing, like dry ice blasting is not abrasive. It does not cause any harm to the environment by emitting or toxic chemicals. In contrast, this method is electrically conductive and require wastewater disposal.
  • Tools for hand. These tools can be used in an environmentally friendly manner, do not use toxic substances, are non-conductive and don't require special waste management. Hand tools have a lot of disadvantages, including abrasiveness and additional labor requirements.

Dry ice blasting offers safety advantages that are not available with other industrial cleaning techniques. These are the most important:

  • Emissions and elimination of toxic chemicals
  • Reduced waste of cleaning solutions
  • Prevention of surface degradation from abrasives

Dry ice is a great way to clean any grime-covered surface without damaging it. Unlike sand, bead blasting, or chemicals, dry ice (CO2) pellets will not leave pits or scratches. CO2 is not a hazardous chemical, unlike most industrial cleaning solvents.

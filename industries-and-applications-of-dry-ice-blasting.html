Industries and Applications of Dry Ice Blasting

Dry ice blasting is an effective solution for removing unwanted contaminants and residues from the production process, such as:

  • Oils
  • Adhesives
  • Resins
  • Paints
  • Greases
  • Asphalt tars
  • Biofilms
  • Aerospace. The aerospace sector relies on dry ice blasting to remove mastic from parts; clean turbine blades; remove labels from fuselage; and clean hydraulics, electrical components and circuitry, and landing gear.
  • Automotive. Dry Ice Blazing is excellent for cleaning undercarriages and engine components, as well as wheels and axles and automotive production equipment.
  • Power plants and electrical equipment. Dry ice blasting is often used to remove oil residue and/or corrosion from power generation equipment, such as turbines and generators. Dry ice blasting is also used in nuclear decontamination and for resin removal in the production of transformers.
  • Fire remediation. After a fire, dry ice blasting can be used to remove any charring or ash from other usable components.
  • Foundries and metal production. This process is used by foundries for cleaning molds and pressing equipment, stripping paint and rust, deburring and cleaning welder robots, residue removal and many other purposes.
  • Mold remediation. Dry ice blasting is a great option for applications that need sanitation.

Here are a few of the benefits to dry ice blasting:

  • No machine disassembly and reassembly is needed - this means companies won't have much unproductive downtime.
  • A quicker clean, that's non-abrasive, non-conductive, non-flammable.
  • There is no secondary waste or cleanup, which saves money on labor and expenses.
  • Companies that have used other methods of cleaning may find it difficult to reach tight areas with our blasting nozzles.
  • Environment-friendly - Meets the requirements of USDA, FDA, and EPA.
  • Safe for employees - no exposure to chemicals or grit media

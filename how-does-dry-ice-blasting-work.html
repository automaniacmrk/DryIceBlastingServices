How Does Dry Ice Blasting Work?

Dry ice blasting is a non-destructive cleaning process that uses solid carbon dioxide to dislodge residue while leaving the surface intact. Similar in concept to pressure washing, dry ice blasting uses compressed air to project media toward a surface at a high velocity. In this case, the media are carbon dioxide particles, which form solid pellets when cooled to -109.3 degrees Fahrenheit. Three immediate results can be obtained by blasting frozen carbon dioxide on a surface:

  • The surface's contents freeze and fracture due to thermal shock before the pellets even make contact.
  • High-speed collisions between pellets and brittle contaminants break down unwanted residue into smaller pieces.
  • Exposure to room temperature air causes the dry ice to rapidly vaporize, resulting in an explosive effect that dislodges more debris.

What is Dry Ice used in Cleaning?

Dry ice blasting and cleaning uses compressed air to push CO2 pellets from a tube using a narrow tip that is aimed at the surface. Although it looks much like pressure washing or sand blasting, the big difference is that dry ice is - dry. Because the CO2 is cold (109 degrees below zero), dirt molecules shrink and are released from the surface. The ice pellets become gas when they hit the surface. This creates a molecular blast that removes dirt, grease, and paint from the surface without significantly cooling it.

  • The only thing left to do is sweep up the residue of paint, ink, resins, asphalt, grime, adhesives or whatever has been removed from surfaces. No waste water. No chemical drainage. No sand or plastic beads scattered around. The CO2 solid pellets dissipate into the air and are absorbed by the atmosphere.
  • There is no dust or detritus from dry ice blasting that can be trapped in or foul up either the machinery being cleaned or nearby equipment.
  • The pellets are non-toxic; pose no workplace hazard to people or equipment, and meet EPA, USDA and FDA guidelines.

This allows you to clean it in situ, which greatly reduces downtime. The dry ice blasting nozzle is agile for reaching most exposed areas. You can clean the entire thing by doing some in-place disassembly if necessary.

Dry Ice Cleaning And Blasting In Industrial Applications Services

Electrical parts and generators can be back in service immediately because you don't have to wait for water or chemicals to dry. Dry ice blasting of radioactive deposits is simpler because the contaminated residue requires far smaller storage containers than would contaminated waste water from pressure washing. Dry ice is being used for cleaning in applications around the world including:

  • Inside Heat Exchanger Tubes - Inside Diameter (ID)
  • Outside Fin Fans Cooler cleaning
  • Fire Restoration and Cleanup
  • Smoke Damage Restoration
  • Presses for printing
  • Manufacturing equipment cleaning
  • Piping Cleaning and Maintenance
  • Storage tanks
  • Reactor Cleaning
  • Shell and Tube Heating exchangers
  • Oil field equipment
  • Turbines, transformers
  • Sub-stations for power
  • Boilers
  • Molds and Presses
  • Electric Motors and Circuit Boards
  • Food Processing Equipment
  • Generators
  • Gears

Industries and commercial companies are also using dry ice blasting to complete periodic maintenance cleaning projects, because of the ability to avoid or isolate downtime. Increasingly the method is being used in the food industry, for safe cleaning of ovens and packaging lines.

Dry ice has replaced solvents that damage the environment and are a workplace hazard, from acids to caustics. It also removes hazardous waste disposal fees that can be very costly because it does not contain the chemical drainage or the residual chemicals.

Our Technology

Dry ice cleaning is non-abrasive, non-conductive, non-toxic, and non-flammable. The ice cleaning process is chemical-free, water-free and doesn't create secondary waste streams. The dry ice blasting machine is incredibly versatile and can be used for a wide range of applications and industries.

The benefits of dry ice blasting

  • Increase efficiency Clean equipment online, while eliminating need for cooldown and disassembly.
  • Secondary waste is not produced by dry ice blasting.
  • Non-abrasive Dry Ice is a media that does not cause damage to substrates.
  • Higher quality parts and less scrap
  • Operator safe Eliminates need for chemicals and repetitive, manual processes.
  • It is non-conductive and toxic. Dry Ice is suitable for commercial usage.
